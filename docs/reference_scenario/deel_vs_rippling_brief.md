## Action Board

### What Rippling should do
- **Mobile Frontline-Worker Workflow Demo: Clock-In-to-Paycheck on the Rippling App** `product_marketing_proof` [medium]
  - Why now: Neither Deel nor Rippling has published evidence of measured mobile time-on-task reduction for hourly/frontline workers. Rippling has an opportunity to be first to prove — not just claim — a mobile experience improvement by shipping a concrete, demonstrable workflow (clock-in, shift swap, pay stub access, time-off request) and measuring actual task completion time on mobile for hourly workers, then publishing the workflow as a proof asset rather than a roadmap announcement.
  - Product comparability: **weak** (Deel mobile app (rebuilt for mobile-specific UX) vs Rippling Workforce Management mobile app)
  - Rippling proof: missing · already-saying-it: underweighted · legal review: True
  - Risk (backfire): If Rippling publishes a mobile workflow demo before the underlying design work (currently only at the hiring stage) is complete, the asset could look rushed or thin next to Deel's more prominent rebuild announcement, and Deel or analysts could point out Rippling is reacting rather than leading — undermining credibility instead of building it.
  - Staged: build_proof → validate_proof → launch_campaign (each with its own proceed/stop rule)
  - Kill rule: If two consecutive test cycles show no measurable task-time improvement or net-negative worker feedback, kill the public-facing proof asset and revert to internal roadmap messaging only
- **"Where Does Your Travel Spend Actually Live?" — A Category-Entry Campaign for Unified Travel & Expense** `demand_gen_campaign` [medium]
  - Why now: Deel is investing distribution effort in surfacing its compliance/employment data inside ChatGPT — a chat-interface convenience play. That's a different buying trigger than the one Rippling can own: the moment a finance or People team realizes travel booking, expense reports, and corporate cards live in three disconnected systems. Rippling has an opportunity to insert itself at that specific trigger moment with Rippling Travel, without claiming superiority over Deel's AI/chat integration (a different capability and unproven-strength area for both sides).
  - Product comparability: **weak** (Deel Compliance/Employment Data via ChatGPT integration vs Rippling Travel (booking + expense + corporate cards))
  - Rippling proof: missing · already-saying-it: underweighted · legal review: True
  - Risk (backfire): Deel could respond by pointing out Rippling has zero quantified proof (customer outcomes, time/cost savings data) for Rippling Travel, while Deel's ChatGPT integration — though a different capability — demonstrates visible AI/product-ecosystem investment. This campaign could be perceived as a distraction if Rippling pushes a "unified travel" narrative without any customer story or savings metric to back the "save time and money" claim, inviting the exact "no proof" critique currently leveled at Deel.
  - Campaign: audience Finance/Controller and Travel & Expense Manager titles at mid-market companies (200-2000 employees) currently using 2+ separate tools for travel booking, expense, and corporate cards, Existing Rippling HR/Payroll customers not yet adopted on Rippling Travel, Companies with distributed or multi-country workforces with recent travel volume growth; channels LinkedIn sponsored content (finance/ops titles), Search ads on category terms (travel and expense management, corporate travel platform), Email nurture to installed base; metric Marketing-qualified leads (MQLs) generated for Rippling Travel, with SQL conversion rate as a secondary gate
  - Staged: build_proof → validate_proof → launch_campaign (each with its own proceed/stop rule)
  - Kill rule: Kill or fully reframe the campaign if, after the minimum sample threshold, CPL exceeds 1.5x blended average AND SQL conversion is below 50% of benchmark for two consecutive reporting cycles, or if no customer proof point materializes within the build_proof stage timeline, preventing any claims stronger than feature description.
- **"Deel vs. Rippling" Sales Battlecard + Objection-Handling Playbook for Consolidation Claims** `competitive_enablement` [medium]
  - Why now: Deel has built strong, well-distributed proof (14 quantified outcomes, 8 named customer stories, compliance certifications) for its all-in-one Global People Platform narrative. Rippling has an equally strong but underweighted equivalent claim — consolidating HR, payroll, IT, and people ops into one platform to eliminate manual busywork and administrative overhead — that is not being systematically armed to sales or surfaced against Deel in competitive deals. The action is to arm AEs/SEs with a rigorous, evidence-based battlecard and objection-handling guide so reps can neutralize Deel's consolidation narrative deal-by-deal, using only substantiated Rippling proof rather than unverified superiority claims.
  - Product comparability: **weak** (Deel Global People Platform (HR, payroll, EOR, compliance across 150 countries) vs Rippling unified HR, Payroll, IT, and people ops platform)
  - Rippling proof: available · already-saying-it: underweighted · legal review: True
  - Risk (backfire): If the battlecard overstates Rippling's global country/compliance coverage to match Deel's 150-country, certification-heavy proof, reps could make claims unsupported by Rippling's evidence base (which here is scoped to financial services HR/payroll/IT consolidation, not verified 150-country EOR compliance). A competitor or prospect fact-check could expose this gap, or Deel could counter-battlecard using its heavier volume of named customer stories and certifications, making Rippling's response look thin on country-specific compliance proof.
  - Staged: build_proof → validate_proof → launch_campaign (each with its own proceed/stop rule)
  - Kill rule: If validation-stage testing shows the comparative claims are repeatedly challenged successfully by prospects or trigger legal/compliance concerns, retire the direct-comparison framing and shift to a non-comparative consolidation proof asset instead.

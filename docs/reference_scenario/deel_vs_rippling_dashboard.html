The Custom Workflow Builder Demo: Show a Live No-Code App Solving a Global Hiring Edge Case Deel's 7 Products Can't Configure product_marketing defensibility: medium
Angle: Deel is marketing breadth — 7 new integrated products announced to cover global hiring, payroll, compliance, and workforce management. But announcing more products is not the same as giving customers the ability to shape any one of them to their actual workflow. Rippling's proof point on this theme is a strong, demonstrable capability: build custom internal apps without code to automate workflow-specific processes. The action is to make that capability tangible and comparable — show, in a live demo, a specific global HR/payroll edge case (e.g., a multi-country approval exception or compliance escalation) being solved via a no-code custom app, and let the buyer judge configurability vs. product-count.
Hypothesis: If Rippling publishes a concrete, demoable proof of its no-code custom app builder solving a global hiring/payroll workflow exception, then prospects evaluating Deel's newly launched 7 products will perceive Rippling as offering a more configurable (not just broader) platform, increasing consideration-stage engagement and sales-assisted win rate in competitive deals against Deel.
Primary metric: Sales-assisted win rate in active Deel-competitive opportunities where the demo/asset was used · guardrails: Deal cycle length in Deel-competitive opportunities, Prospect-reported confusion or pushback flagged by sales, Legal/compliance flags on comparative claims post-launch · min sample: At least 15 sales-assisted opportunities where the demo/asset was used against Deel before evaluating win-rate impact
Iterate rule: If buyer feedback indicates the workflow example chosen doesn't map to their most pressing pain point, iterate the demo with alternate workflow scenarios (e.g., compliance escalation vs. approval routing) before re-testing
Scale rule: If win rate in Deel-competitive deals improves by a meaningful margin (e.g., double-digit percentage point lift) with no legal/compliance issues after the minimum sample, expand distribution to broader campaign channels and analyst briefings
Backfire risk: Deel could respond by showcasing its own configurability features (if any exist but weren't in this evidence set), reframing the comparison as apples-to-apples and neutralizing the differentiation. Additionally, because comparability is weak (product breadth vs. configurability are different value axes), a buyer or analyst could call out the comparison as a deflection rather than a direct rebuttal to Deel's breadth claim — Rippling must be careful to position this as "a different lens on complexity" rather than implying its custom app builder makes Deel's 7 products redundant.
Kill rule: If validate_proof stage surfaces credible evidence that Deel has an equivalent no-code configurability capability, or if legal review cannot clear the asset without diluting it into a non-differentiated statement, kill the campaign-launch stage and revert to internal enablement only
build proof — Produce a demo-grade workflow page and video showing a real (or realistic composite) customer using Rippling's no-code app builder to solve a specific global hiring/payroll exception (e.g., country-specific approval routing, compliance escalation).
✓ proceed if: A customer or sales-engineering example exists that can be shown end-to-end without fabrication; Legal/comparative-claims review confirms no direct superiority language is used; The demo clearly maps to a job Deel's 7-product announcement claims to solve
✕ stop/reframe if: No real customer or credible composite workflow can be found to demo; Legal flags the framing as an implied superiority claim that can't be substantiated
validate proof — Test the demo/page with a small set of target buyers (analysts, existing customers in eval cycles, sales calls) to see if the configurability angle resonates and holds up against Deel comparison questions.
✓ proceed if: Buyers in live sales cycles reference the demo positively as a differentiator; No material factual pushback from prospects who have evaluated Deel's new products directly
✕ stop/reframe if: Prospects report Deel has comparable configurability not reflected in our evidence; Sales reports the angle causes confusion rather than clarity about Rippling's value
launch campaign — Publish the workflow/demo page broadly and equip sales with the asset for competitive deals against Deel in active evaluations.
✓ proceed if: Validate_proof stage shows positive buyer reaction and no factual contradiction; Sales engineering confirms the demo is repeatable across multiple prospect conversations
✕ stop/reframe if: Deel publicly launches a comparable no-code/configurability capability that undermines the differentiation; Win-rate or deal-cycle data shows no measurable impact after a full sales cycle of use
"Certified, Not Just Claimed": IT Security Demand-Gen Push Targeting Deel Endpoint-Security Claims product_marketing defensibility: medium
Angle: Deel IT's endpoint protection claims are backed mostly by feature assertions and a single customer story, not third-party certification. Rippling can lead with its documented industry certifications and regular audits as the evidence-backed reason security-conscious IT buyers should scrutinize vendor security claims before trusting device-lifecycle security to a payroll-first HR platform.
Hypothesis: If Rippling promotes its certified, audited security posture as a buyer-education angle (rather than a direct competitive claim), IT/security personas evaluating global HR/IT platforms will engage at a higher rate and convert to qualified pipeline more efficiently than generic security messaging, because the underweighted proof point is genuinely differentiated on evidence strength.
Primary metric: Marketing-qualified leads (MQLs) from IT/security persona segment · guardrails: Cost per qualified lead vs. existing demand-gen benchmark, Landing page bounce rate, Sales-reported lead quality/intent score, Legal/compliance complaint or challenge rate · min sample: Pilot must reach at least 5,000 ad impressions and 50 landing page sessions per channel before evaluating engagement metrics for statistical reliability.
Iterate rule: If engagement is moderate but conversion to qualified lead is weak, revise landing page checklist and CTA copy, and retest with the same audience before altering channel mix.
Scale rule: Scale spend and expand to additional channels only if pilot cost-per-qualified-lead is at or below existing IT/security demand-gen benchmark and sales confirms lead quality over a 2-week minimum follow-up window.
Backfire risk: Deel may already hold relevant certifications not captured in the supplied evidence (the gap reflects observed proof distribution, not an audited absence of certification) — if Deel publishes or already has equivalent certification claims, Rippling's implicit "more substantiated" framing could look either redundant or, worse, factually wrong, inviting a direct rebuttal. The campaign must avoid stating or implying Deel is uncertified.
Kill rule: Kill or fully reframe the campaign if legal/compliance identifies claim risk during the pilot, if cost-per-qualified-lead exceeds benchmark by more than 50% after two optimization cycles, or if Deel publicly demonstrates equivalent/superior certification evidence that undermines the campaign's premise.
build proof — Confirm and document Rippling's specific certifications, audit cadence, and device-lifecycle security policy evidence in a form usable in ad claims and landing page copy.
✓ proceed if: Rippling has named, verifiable certifications (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001) with current audit dates available for public reference; Legal/compliance confirms specific claims are substantiated and safe for comparative-adjacent framing
✕ stop/reframe if: Certifications are outdated, in-progress, or cannot be publicly cited; Legal flags that current proof cannot support even non-comparative security claims
validate proof — Test message resonance and claim clarity with a small paid pilot before scaling spend.
✓ proceed if: CTR and landing page engagement on security-certification messaging meet or exceed account benchmark for IT/security campaigns; No legal or customer pushback on claim framing during pilot window
✕ stop/reframe if: Engagement is materially below benchmark, indicating the angle doesn't resonate; Prospects or reviewers challenge the accuracy or fairness of the comparative framing
launch campaign — Scale the validated campaign across full channel mix to IT/security personas in mid-market segment.
✓ proceed if: Pilot cost-per-qualified-lead is at or below existing paid demand-gen benchmarks; Sales reports leads are qualified and referencing the security/certification message positively
✕ stop/reframe if: Cost-per-qualified-lead exceeds benchmark by a significant margin after full ramp; Sales feedback indicates leads are low-intent or message causes confusion about product scope
Deel Goals/Engage Objection-Handling Battlecard + Discovery Guide for Workforce Visibility Deals product_marketing defensibility: low
Angle: When Deel reps lead with "Goals in Engage gives you built-in goal-setting and alignment tracking across the org, without manual effort," Rippling's field team needs a structured way to probe the actual evidence behind that claim (which today is thin: two feature assertions, one customer story, one quantified outcome, one unsupported assertion) rather than concede the ground or overclaim Rippling's own unified position. The angle is not "Rippling is better at goals tracking" — Rippling has no comparable proof point on this theme yet — but rather "let's test whether Deel's alignment claim holds up under the customer's actual multi-level, multi-country reporting workflow," using discovery questions that surface gaps distributors of HR software commonly find in point/expansion modules bolted onto payroll-and-EOR cores.
Hypothesis: If Rippling AEs are equipped with a structured discovery-question battlecard targeting the specific weak points in Deel's Goals/Engage proof (feature assertions without depth, one customer story, one quantified outcome, one unsupported assertion), then reps will more effectively neutralize the objection in live deals without resorting to unsupported competitive claims, improving deal confidence and progression on workforce-visibility-themed opportunities.
Primary metric: Rep-reported objection-handling success rate (deal advanced / objection neutralized) on deals where Deel Goals/Engage is raised · guardrails: Number of legal/compliance flags on battlecard language, Rep confidence score (post-training survey), Deal cycle length for affected opportunities (should not increase) · min sample: At least 15 tracked deal instances where the Deel Goals/Engage objection is raised and the battlecard is used, before evaluating success rate
Iterate rule: If success rate is between 25-50% or reps request different framing/questions, revise discovery questions and re-pilot with a fresh set of deals for one more cycle before deciding to scale or kill
Scale rule: If pilot shows >=50% improvement in objection neutralization rate and zero unresolved legal flags across the minimum sample, roll out to full sales org via SKO and CRM embed
Backfire risk: If reps use the battlecard to imply Rippling has superior or equivalent goal-tracking capability without any shipped proof, prospects or Deel could call out the bluff, and it could surface publicly as an unsubstantiated claim, damaging trust. There is also risk that probing Deel's "weak proof" comes across as deflection if Rippling cannot show its own roadmap or capability in this area when directly asked.
Kill rule: If fewer than 25% of piloted deals show improved objection handling, or if legal/compliance identifies unresolvable risk in the claims framing, retire the asset and default reps to a neutral 'we don't have a comparable proof point yet' response
build proof — Interview 5-8 AEs/SEs who have faced the Deel Goals/Engage objection in live deals; compile actual prospect questions and Deel's stated capability language into a discovery-question battlecard draft, and confirm with Rippling product team what (if anything) exists today for goal-tracking/alignment features.
✓ proceed if: At least 3 independent deal instances confirm the objection is recurring and costing momentum in deals; Product team confirms current state (missing/partial/available) of any comparable Rippling capability so reps are not left exposed
✕ stop/reframe if: Fewer than 2 reps report ever encountering this objection in the last quarter, suggesting low deal relevance; Product team reveals a roadmap commitment that would make a full feature-comparison asset more appropriate than a discovery-only battlecard
validate proof — Pilot the battlecard/discovery guide with a small group of AEs (5-10) on live deals where the Deel goals objection surfaces; track whether the discovery questions help reps advance or neutralize the objection without making unsupported claims.
✓ proceed if: Pilot reps report the discovery questions successfully shift the conversation away from feature-parity claims toward the prospect's actual requirements in at least half of tracked deals; No legal/compliance flags raised on the language used
✕ stop/reframe if: Reps report prospects push back hard when Rippling cannot answer 'what do you offer instead,' damaging credibility; Legal flags any claim in the asset as unsupported or risky
launch campaign — Roll out the finalized battlecard and discovery guide org-wide via enablement platform and SKO training, with a feedback loop for reps to flag new Deel claims or proof points as they emerge.
✓ proceed if: Validation stage shows measurable improvement in deal progression rate or objection-handling confidence scores; Enablement leadership signs off on broad distribution
✕ stop/reframe if: Deel materially strengthens its proof (e.g., adds multiple named case studies or third-party validation) such that the battlecard's premise of 'weak proof' becomes outdated; Sales feedback indicates the asset is rarely used or not moving deals

## Industry lens

- **Category:** global employment / EOR (employer of record) and global payroll platforms — unified global payroll, HR & compliance platform for distributed/international workforces (incl. contractor payments and IT/equipment provisioning)
- **Buyer terminology in this category:** Employer of Record (EOR), global payroll, compliant hiring, contractor management, worker lifecycle, global people platform, multi-country compliance, cross-border payments
- **Who buys here:** VP/Head of People or HR Operations, Global Payroll Director, CFO / Finance leader overseeing global workforce cost, COO or VP of Operations scaling international teams, IT Director responsible for global equipment/device provisioning, General Counsel or Compliance lead for multi-country employment risk
- **How Rippling competes in this category:** Based on Rippling's multi-product platform positioning, Rippling competes in this category not as a pure EOR/global-payroll point solution but as a unified system of record for employees, contractors, devices, and apps that happens to also handle global payroll and compliance — meaning the relevant contrast isn't just 'more countries covered' but 'one true source of workforce data' driving HR, IT, and finance workflows together, versus Deel's narrower framing as a specialized global execution/EOR layer that sits atop or alongside a company's existing systems.
- **Positioning frame for Rippling:** Reframe the buying decision from 'best global payroll/EOR execution layer' to 'unified workforce system of record' — position Rippling as eliminating fragmentation not just across countries and worker types (the EOR pitch) but across HR, IT, finance, and device/app management simultaneously, so global compliance and payroll are one automated output of a single employee data model rather than a separate consolidated platform bolted onto the rest of the business.
